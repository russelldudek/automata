Entry principle

Earn the right
to standardize.

The objective is not to import an operating template. It is to establish enough shared truth that Automata can preserve what clients value, remove avoidable friction, and scale evidence-backed patterns.

Days 0-30 - Establish the truth

  • Listen with founders/executives, sales, delivery, technical teams, client success, finance, operations, and representative clients.
  • Build a portfolio inventory linking client promise, workflow, automation, owner, run state, exception history, adoption, value evidence, support burden, and reuse potential.
  • Map the critical handoffs and identify where context, authority, or acceptance evidence is lost.
  • Review the revenue model, pricing, backlog, capacity, utilization, platform/model costs, delivery cycle, support load, cash mechanics, and financial reporting definitions.
  • Document immediate client, delivery, legal, security, quality, workforce, and business-continuity risks.

Outputs

  • Company listening map
  • Portfolio and operating baseline
  • Decision-rights inventory
  • Financial and capacity question set
  • Immediate-risk register

Do not do yet

  • Reorganize on anecdote
  • Declare arbitrary targets
  • Standardize legitimate client differentiation
  • Add dashboards before agreeing on definitions
  • Automate a broken handoff

Days 31-60 - Clarify the operating contract

  • Define a service architecture that distinguishes discovery, custom build, reusable component, managed run/support, advisory, and productized offerings where appropriate.
  • Clarify decision rights for scope, design, model/tool choice, human authority, acceptance, incident response, client communication, change requests, and stop/hold decisions.
  • Agree on the minimum evidence required before work begins, is accepted, enters recurring operation, expands, or is reused.
  • Define a small balanced scorecard spanning client value, run health, adoption, economics, and learning.
  • Identify legitimate variation versus repeated failure modes and avoidable customization.

Days 61-90 - Run the cadence

  • Launch the Automation Yield Review: a weekly decision forum connecting pipeline, capacity, delivery, recurring run health, client outcomes, financial drivers, incidents, and reusable learning.
  • Introduce a clear portfolio state for commit, watch, hold, recover, productize, or stop - with owner and next evidence.
  • Install a blameless incident-learning mechanism that links failure mode, human authority, recovery, client communication, recurrence risk, and operating-rule change.
  • Connect workforce planning and development to the service model, capability gaps, expected demand, and leadership load.
  • Move recurring executive decisions into explicit mechanisms without stripping teams of judgment.

Days 91-120 - Scale the patterns

  • Select repeated, evidence-backed work for reusable components, playbooks, training, managed services, or productization.
  • Align team structure, leadership roles, hiring, development, and external partners to the service architecture and demand model.
  • Set quarterly operating priorities and guardrails for client outcomes, capacity, margin drivers, reliability, adoption, reuse, and risk.
  • Close or redesign the mechanisms that create activity without decision value.
  • Present the operating roadmap with explicit assumptions, unresolved questions, owners, and proof points.

120-day outcomes

Shared truth

Leadership and teams use common definitions for the portfolio, performance, risk, economics, and ownership.

Clear authority

Decisions are made at the right level, with human judgment and escalation explicit.

Evidence-based cadence

Reviews produce decisions, owners, and learning rather than status narration.

Capacity visibility

Demand, staffing, delivery load, support burden, and critical skills can be discussed together.

Pattern portfolio

Repeated work has a path to reuse or productization; exceptions have a containment path.

Operating roadmap

The next two quarters are framed as measurable bets with assumptions and proof points.
