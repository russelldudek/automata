# Automata Intelligence Brand Intelligence

## Scope and evidence status

This campaign uses the official company website and the role description supplied in the application context as the authoritative public identity and role sources available during production.

- Official company source: https://automataintelligence.ai/
- Public page title observed: `Automata Intelligence - Powering Intelligent Transformation`
- Role source: Chief Operating Officer description supplied directly in the campaign request
- Public job-posting URL: not available; the supplied URL is the company website

## Official logo and brand assets

A sufficiently crisp, unambiguous official logo/wordmark asset could not be retrieved from the accessible public website during production. Image-search results were rejected because they were ambiguous or belonged to unrelated organizations. No logo was traced, redrawn, approximated, or sourced from a third-party logo aggregator.

Visible company identity therefore uses the clearly typeset name `Automata Intelligence` with the qualifier `Candidate vision for Chief Operating Officer by Russell Dudek`. This is not represented as an official wordmark.

`assets/brand/` contains a provenance note rather than a fabricated mark.

## Color token provenance

No official color tokens or stable stylesheet values could be retrieved. All campaign colors are therefore classified as `candidate-original` and provisional:

- Graphite: `#101312`
- Warm paper: `#F4F1E8`
- Signal lime: `#C9F24B`
- Amber evidence state: `#FFB000`
- Coral exception state: `#FF725E`
- Teal authority state: `#5AD7C8`

These colors express the role-derived cellular-automation/state-transition metaphor. They must not be described as Automata Intelligence's official palette.

## Typography decision

No official typeface evidence was retrievable. The campaign uses a licensed/local-first stack centered on DejaVu Sans and Segoe UI, with DejaVu Sans Mono/Cascadia Mono for operating-state labels. The choice is provisional and designed for technical clarity, accessibility, and reliable PDF rendering. No proprietary font files are copied or committed.

## Photography, illustration, and interface cues

No official photography or interface assets are used. The core visual is an original Operating Rulefield derived from the company's automation context and the COO mandate. It is not a recreation of an internal Automata platform or dashboard.

## Motion and geometry

- Square-cell geometry represents recurring automation units operating under local rules.
- State transitions represent promise, authority, active run, evidence, reuse, and exception.
- Motion propagates across the field to explain how operating rules compound across a growing portfolio.
- Reduced-motion mode preserves all state labels, controls, and readouts without animation.

## Artifact application

- Candidate vision: full Operating Rulefield visual system and interactive scenarios
- Resume and cover letter: restrained graphite/paper/signal treatment with clearly typeset company name
- Interview brief and 120-day plan: same rules/evidence grammar with conservative print composition
- Automation Yield Review: operating tables and decision states using the same visual vocabulary

## Independent-candidate distinction

Every candidate-facing surface identifies the work as an independent candidate vision or candidate material by Russell Dudek. The campaign does not imply endorsement, authorship, employment, or official publication by Automata Intelligence.

## Completion limitation

Brand fidelity remains blocked pending access to a verifiable official logo/wordmark, current brand colors and typography evidence, and a rendered comparison against the official company experience. The campaign intentionally records this limitation rather than inventing fidelity.
